Basic Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 375 degrees F. Boil ziti in salted water for 2 minutes less than the package says. Drain.
  2. Brown sausage in a skillet, breaking it into small pieces. Add olive oil only if the pan is dry.
  3. Add onion and cook until softened, 4 to 5 minutes. Add garlic for 30 seconds.
  4. Stir in marinara, crushed tomatoes, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer 8 to 10 minutes.
  5. Mix ricotta, egg, 1 cup mozzarella, 1/4 cup Parmesan, and parsley in a bowl.
  6. Combine pasta with most of the sauce. Layer half the pasta in a 9x13 dish, dollop with ricotta mixture, then add remaining pasta. Top with remaining sauce, 1 cup mozzarella, and 1/4 cup Parmesan.
  7. Cover with foil and bake 20 minutes. Uncover and bake 10 to 15 minutes more, until bubbling and browned in spots. Rest 10 minutes before serving.
  8. For garlic sourdough, mix softened butter, garlic, parsley, and salt. Spread on sliced sourdough and bake on a sheet pan for 8 to 10 minutes, until toasted.

Notes

  • What might need testing: Whether the family prefers more sauce or more cheese.
  • Possible substitutions: Use hot Italian sausage for more heat, or half mild and half hot.
  • Prep-ahead ideas: Assemble ziti earlier Saturday and refrigerate. Add 10 to 15 minutes to covered bake time if cold from the fridge.
  • Family preference concerns: This is heavier than the rice bowls, so serve with a simple salad next time if desired.
