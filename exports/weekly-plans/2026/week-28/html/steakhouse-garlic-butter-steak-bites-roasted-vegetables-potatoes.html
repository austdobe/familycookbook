Basic Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 425 degrees F. Boil halved baby potatoes in salted water for 8 to 10 minutes, until just tender. Drain well.
  2. Toss potatoes with 2 tbsp olive oil, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp pepper. Roast cut-side down for 25 to 30 minutes, flipping once, until crisp and browned.
  3. Toss mushrooms, zucchini, and summer squash with 2 tbsp olive oil, thyme, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp pepper. Roast on a separate area or second sheet pan for 18 to 22 minutes, until browned at the edges.
  4. Pat steak bites very dry. Season with steak seasoning, 1/2 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp pepper, and Worcestershire.
  5. Heat neutral oil in a heavy skillet over medium-high heat. Sear steak bites in batches 2 to 3 minutes per side, until browned. Avoid crowding.
  6. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add butter and garlic to the skillet, then return steak bites and toss 30 to 60 seconds until coated. Pull steak around 130 to 135 degrees F for medium-rare or 140 to 145 degrees F for medium.
  7. Finish steak with parsley and serve with crispy potatoes and roasted vegetables.

Notes

  • What might need testing: Preferred steak doneness and whether potatoes need more crisping time.
  • Possible substitutions: Use pre-cut steak tips if they look fresh and evenly sized.
  • Prep-ahead ideas: Cut vegetables earlier Friday and refrigerate dry. Do not cut potatoes too early unless stored in cold water and dried very well.
  • Family preference concerns: Keep garlic butter separate for anyone who wants lighter sauce.
