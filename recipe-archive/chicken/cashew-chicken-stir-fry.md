# Cashew Chicken Stir Fry

## Planning Summary

- Servings: 4
- Estimated prep time: 20 minutes
- Estimated cook time: 20 minutes
- Protein: Chicken breast
- Cuisine or flavor direction: Chinese-American stir fry, cashews, soy-honey sauce
- Best day to cook: Early or midweek
- Perishability notes: Uses chicken and several fresh vegetables. Cook before snap peas and broccoli lose freshness.

## Equipment

- Wok or large skillet
- Mixing bowl
- Cutting board and knife
- Spoon or spatula

## Basic Instructions

1. Cook rice according to package directions if serving with rice.
2. Whisk soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, arrowroot, and water.
3. Heat oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken in batches until browned and cooked to 165 degrees F. Transfer to a plate.
4. Stir fry bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, and snap peas until crisp-tender, 5 to 7 minutes.
5. Return chicken to the pan. Add sauce and simmer 1 to 3 minutes, until glossy and thickened.
6. Stir in cashews right before serving.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Sauce sweetness and vegetable mix.
- Possible substitutions: Chicken thighs stay juicier if stir-fry timing is inconsistent.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Slice chicken and vegetables the morning of cooking.
- Family preference concerns: Add cashews at the end so they stay crisp.
