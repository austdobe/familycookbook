# Jamaican Jerk Chicken with Coconut Rice and Pineapple Mango Salsa

## Planning Summary

- Servings: 4
- Estimated prep time: 30 minutes, plus 30 minutes marinade time
- Estimated cook time: 35 minutes
- Protein: Chicken thighs
- Cuisine or flavor direction: Jamaican-inspired jerk, smoky, sweet, spicy, citrusy
- Best day to cook: Sunday
- Perishability notes: Uses fresh chicken, mango, pineapple, cilantro, lime, and peppers. Best early in the week.

## Basic Instructions

1. Blend or finely mash the marinade ingredients: oil, soy sauce, vinegar, lime juice, brown sugar, green onions, garlic, ginger, chile pepper, allspice, thyme, smoked paprika, cinnamon, nutmeg, cayenne, salt, and black pepper.
2. Coat chicken with the marinade. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes, or up to 8 hours.
3. Make the salsa by mixing pineapple, mango, bell pepper, red onion, cilantro, jalapeno, lime juice, and salt. Refrigerate until serving.
4. Rinse rice until the water runs mostly clear. Add rice, coconut milk, water, and salt to a saucepan. Bring to a simmer, cover, reduce heat to low, and cook 15 minutes. Rest covered 10 minutes, then fluff with lime juice.
5. Grill chicken over medium-high heat for 5 to 7 minutes per side, until browned and the thickest pieces reach 165 degrees F. Rest 5 minutes before slicing.
6. Serve sliced jerk chicken over coconut rice with pineapple mango salsa on top.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Family heat tolerance. Start with half a Scotch bonnet or use jalapeno if needed.
- Possible substitutions: Chicken breasts can work but may dry out faster.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Make marinade and salsa Sunday morning. Do not add salsa salt until closer to serving if the fruit is very juicy.
- Family preference concerns: Smoky heat may need a mild portion set aside before adding cayenne or hot pepper.
