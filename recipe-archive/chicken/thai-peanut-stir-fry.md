## Planning Summary

- Servings: 4
- Estimated prep time: 20 minutes
- Estimated cook time: 20 minutes
- Protein: Chicken or tofu
- Cuisine or flavor direction: Thai-inspired peanut sauce, savory-sweet, ginger-garlic
- Best day to cook: Midweek
- Perishability notes: Uses chicken or tofu plus broccoli, peppers, and carrots. Cook fresh vegetables before they soften.

## Equipment

- Wok or large skillet
- Mixing bowl
- Cutting board and knife
- Spoon or spatula

## Basic Instructions

1. Cook rice according to package directions if serving with rice.
2. Whisk peanut butter, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, arrowroot, and warm water until smooth.
3. Heat oil in a wok. Cook chicken until browned and 165 degrees F, or cook tofu until golden. Transfer to a plate.
4. Stir fry broccoli, bell peppers, and carrots until crisp-tender.
5. Return protein to the pan. Add peanut sauce and simmer 2 to 4 minutes, until thickened and coating everything.
6. Serve over rice.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Sauce thickness and peanut intensity.
- Possible substitutions: Coconut milk can replace part of the water for a richer sauce.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Make sauce earlier the same day and refrigerate.
- Family preference concerns: Peanut sauce thickens quickly; loosen with water if it gets pasty.
