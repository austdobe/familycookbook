## Basic Instructions

1. Start rice: rinse jasmine rice, then simmer with water and salt for 15 minutes. Rest covered 10 minutes.
2. Toss sliced beef with arrowroot until lightly coated.
3. Whisk soy sauce, water, brown sugar, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes in a bowl.
4. Stir-fry broccoli in 1 tbsp oil with salt until bright green and crisp-tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
5. Sear beef in batches in 3 tbsp oil over medium-high heat. Let pieces brown before turning so the coating gets lightly crisp.
6. Add garlic and ginger for 30 seconds. Pour in sauce and simmer 2 to 4 minutes, until glossy and thick enough to coat the beef.
7. Serve beef and broccoli over rice. Top with green onions.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Sauce sweetness. Reduce brown sugar to 1/4 cup if the family wants it less sweet.
- Possible substitutions: Chicken can be used, but cook to 165 degrees F.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Slice beef and broccoli the night before. Keep beef uncovered or loosely covered in the fridge for easier searing.
- Family preference concerns: Broccoli texture matters; keep it crisp-tender, not soft.
