## Basic Instructions

1. Mix beef, egg, panko, milk, Worcestershire, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Shape into 16 to 20 meatballs.
2. Brown meatballs in olive oil in a deep skillet. They do not need to cook through yet. Transfer to a plate.
3. Add butter and onions to the skillet. Cook over medium heat 18 to 25 minutes, stirring often, until onions are deep golden and soft. Add thyme.
4. Add beef broth and Worcestershire. Return meatballs to the skillet, cover, and simmer 12 to 15 minutes, until meatballs reach 160 degrees F.
5. Stir arrowroot with cold water. Add to the simmering gravy and cook 1 to 2 minutes, until glossy and thickened. Add cheese over the meatballs if using, cover briefly, and let melt.
6. Meanwhile, boil potatoes in salted water until fork-tender, about 15 minutes. Drain and mash with butter, milk, sour cream, and salt.
7. Saute green beans in olive oil until crisp-tender, 6 to 8 minutes. Add garlic for the last minute, then finish with lemon juice and salt.
8. Serve meatballs and gravy over mashed potatoes with green beans on the side.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Whether the onion gravy is rich enough without cheese.
- Possible substitutions: Swiss is more budget-friendly than Gruyere.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Shape meatballs earlier in the day and refrigerate.
- Family preference concerns: Onion texture may matter; cook onions very soft for a smoother gravy feel.
