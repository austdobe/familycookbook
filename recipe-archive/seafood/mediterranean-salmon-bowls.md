# Mediterranean Salmon Bowls

## Planning Summary

- Servings: 4
- Estimated prep time: 20 minutes
- Estimated cook time: 20 minutes
- Protein: Salmon
- Cuisine or flavor direction: Mediterranean bowl, lemon yogurt, cucumber, feta, olives
- Best day to cook: Early week
- Perishability notes: Uses salmon, cucumber, tomatoes, feta, and yogurt. Cook salmon quickly after shopping.

## Equipment

- Baking sheet
- Saucepan
- Mixing bowl
- Cutting board and knife

## Ingredients

Assume nothing is on hand. Include quantities for everything used.

| Quantity | Ingredient | Preferred version/type | Acceptable alternatives | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 1/2 lb | Salmon | Skin-on or skinless fillet | Frozen salmon, thawed | Main protein |
| 1 tbsp | Olive oil | Extra-virgin or regular | Avocado oil | Salmon |
| 1 tsp | Kosher salt | Kosher | Fine sea salt, use less | Divided |
| 1/2 tsp | Black pepper | Ground | Freshly ground | Divided |
| 1 1/2 cups dry | Jasmine rice | Long-grain jasmine | Brown rice or quinoa | Bowl base |
| 1 | English cucumber | Diced | 2 Persian cucumbers | Topping |
| 1 pint | Grape tomatoes | Halved | Cherry tomatoes | Topping |
| 1/2 | Red onion | Thinly sliced | Shallot | Topping |
| 1/2 cup | Feta cheese | Crumbled | Goat cheese | Topping |
| 1/3 cup | Kalamata olives | Pitted, sliced | Green olives | Topping |
| 1 cup | Plain Greek yogurt | Full-fat or 2 percent | Plain yogurt, strained | Sauce |
| 1 | Lemon | Juiced | Bottled lemon juice | Sauce |
| 1 clove | Garlic | Fresh minced | 1/4 tsp garlic powder | Sauce |

## Basic Instructions

1. Heat oven to 400 degrees F. Place salmon on a baking sheet and rub with olive oil, half the salt, and half the pepper.
2. Bake salmon 12 to 15 minutes, until it flakes easily.
3. Cook rice according to package directions.
4. Mix Greek yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, remaining salt, and remaining pepper.
5. Prepare cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, feta, and olives.
6. Assemble bowls with rice, salmon, vegetables, feta, olives, and lemon yogurt sauce.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Whether rice, quinoa, or greens are the best base.
- Possible substitutions: Chicken can replace salmon for a non-seafood version.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Make sauce and chop vegetables earlier the same day.
- Family preference concerns: Keep olives separate if they are polarizing.
