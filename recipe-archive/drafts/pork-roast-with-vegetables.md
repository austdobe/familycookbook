## Basic Instructions

1. Heat oven to 325 degrees F. Pat pork dry and season with salt, pepper, rosemary, and thyme.
2. Heat oil in a Dutch oven and brown pork on all sides, 8 to 10 minutes total.
3. Add potatoes, carrots, onion, garlic, and chicken broth around the pork.
4. Cover and roast until tender. Pork shoulder may need 3 to 3 1/2 hours; pork loin may need 1 1/2 to 2 hours and should be pulled around 145 degrees F.
5. Rest pork 10 to 15 minutes. Slice or shred depending on cut.
6. Serve pork with vegetables and pan juices.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Best pork cut for the family: sliceable loin versus shreddable shoulder.
- Possible substitutions: Add celery or parsnips if desired.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Cut vegetables the morning of cooking.
- Family preference concerns: Pork loin dries out if cooked like pork shoulder; check temperature early.
