## Planning Summary

- Servings: 4
- Estimated prep time: 10 minutes
- Estimated cook time: 0 minutes
- Protein: Greek yogurt
- Cuisine or flavor direction: Light dessert, peaches, honey, cinnamon
- Best day to cook: Any night
- Perishability notes: Uses fresh peaches and Greek yogurt. Best when peaches are ripe.

## Equipment

- Bowls
- Cutting board and knife

## Ingredients

Assume nothing is on hand. Include quantities for everything used.

| Quantity | Ingredient | Preferred version/type | Acceptable alternatives | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 2 cups | Greek yogurt | Plain full-fat or 2 percent | Vanilla Greek yogurt, reduce honey | Base |
| 4 | Fresh peaches | Ripe but not mushy | Canned peaches, drained | Fruit |
| 2 tbsp | Honey | Liquid honey | Maple syrup | Sweetener |
| 1/2 tsp | Cinnamon | Ground | Pumpkin pie spice | Seasoning |
| 1/2 cup | Granola | Crunchy | Chopped nuts | Optional topping |

## Basic Instructions

1. Slice peaches.
2. Divide Greek yogurt among bowls.
3. Top with peaches, honey, and cinnamon.
4. Add granola only right before serving so it stays crunchy.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Whether plain yogurt needs more honey for dessert.
- Possible substitutions: Use berries, nectarines, or thawed frozen peaches.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Portion yogurt ahead; slice peaches close to serving.
- Family preference concerns: Granola gets soft if added too early.
