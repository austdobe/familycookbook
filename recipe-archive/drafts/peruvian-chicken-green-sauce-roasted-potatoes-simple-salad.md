## Basic Instructions

1. Mix oil, lime juice, garlic, soy sauce, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Coat chicken thoroughly and refrigerate at least 2 hours, or up to overnight.
2. Heat grill to medium, or heat oven to 425 degrees F if roasting. Start potatoes first by tossing wedges with oil, salt, and pepper. Roast 35 to 45 minutes, turning once, until browned and tender.
3. Grill chicken skin-side down over medium heat until browned, then move to indirect heat and cook until the thickest pieces reach 165 degrees F. If roasting, place chicken on a sheet pan and roast 40 to 50 minutes.
4. Blend cilantro, jalapeno, mayonnaise, yogurt, garlic, lime juice, and salt until smooth. Taste and adjust lime or salt.
5. Toss romaine, tomatoes, and red onion with olive oil, lime juice, and salt right before serving.
6. Serve chicken with roasted potatoes, salad, and plenty of green sauce.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Green sauce heat level and whether the family prefers yogurt-heavy or mayo-heavy sauce.
- Possible substitutions: Boneless thighs work for faster cooking, but check early and pull at 165 degrees F.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Make marinade and green sauce Sunday morning. Roast potatoes day-of for best texture.
- Family preference concerns: Keep some sauce mild by blending without jalapeno first, then adding jalapeno to the rest.
