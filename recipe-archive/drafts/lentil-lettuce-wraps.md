## Planning Summary

- Servings: 4
- Estimated prep time: 15 minutes
- Estimated cook time: 25 minutes
- Protein: Red lentils with optional ground beef
- Cuisine or flavor direction: Lettuce wraps, soy-ginger, savory
- Best day to cook: Any weeknight
- Perishability notes: Uses lettuce, onion, garlic, and carrots. Lettuce should be used while crisp.

## Equipment

- Saucepan
- Skillet
- Cutting board and knife
- Spoon or spatula

## Ingredients

Assume nothing is on hand. Include quantities for everything used.

| Quantity | Ingredient | Preferred version/type | Acceptable alternatives | Notes |

| 1 cup | Red lentils | Dry red lentils | Brown lentils, longer cook time | Base |
| 2 cups | Broth | Low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth | Water plus salt | Lentils |

| 1 tbsp | Neutral oil | Avocado or canola oil | Vegetable oil | Cooking |
| 1 head | Butter lettuce | Whole leaves | Romaine leaves | Serving |

## Basic Instructions

1. Simmer lentils with broth until soft and thick, 12 to 15 minutes.
2. If using beef, brown it in a skillet and drain excess grease if needed.
3. Add oil, onion, carrots, garlic, and ginger to the skillet. Cook until vegetables soften, 4 to 6 minutes.
4. Stir in cooked lentils, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Simmer until thick enough to spoon into lettuce leaves.
5. Spoon filling into butter lettuce leaves and serve warm.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Whether the family prefers lentil-only or beef-lentil filling.
- Possible substitutions: Ground turkey works for a lighter version.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Cook lentils ahead and refrigerate up to 2 days.
- Family preference concerns: Filling needs to be thick, not soupy, or lettuce wraps will be messy.
