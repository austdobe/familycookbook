## Planning Summary

- Servings: 4
- Estimated prep time: 15 minutes

## Basic Instructions

1. Start rice by rinsing jasmine rice, then simmering with water and salt for 15 minutes. Rest covered 10 minutes.
2. Heat oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven. Brown chicken in batches until lightly browned, then transfer to a plate.
3. Cook onion until softened, 4 to 6 minutes. Add garlic and ginger and cook 30 seconds.
4. Stir in tomato paste, paprika, garam masala, cumin, turmeric, cayenne, and salt. Cook 1 minute.
5. Add coconut milk and browned chicken. Simmer 18 to 22 minutes, until chicken reaches 165 degrees F and sauce is flavorful.
6. If a thicker sauce is desired, mix arrowroot with cold water and stir into the simmering sauce for 1 to 2 minutes.
7. Serve over rice.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Sauce thickness and heat level.
- Possible substitutions: Heavy cream can replace coconut milk for a richer non-coconut version.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Dice onion and chicken the night before; keep separate.
- Family preference concerns: Start without cayenne for a family-friendly baseline.
