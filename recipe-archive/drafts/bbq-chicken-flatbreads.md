## Basic Instructions

1. Heat oven to 425 degrees F. Season chicken with salt, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper.
2. Cook chicken in oil in a skillet over medium-high heat until browned and cooked to 165 degrees F, 5 to 7 minutes per side depending on thickness. Rest 5 minutes, then chop or shred.
3. Toss chicken with 1/2 cup BBQ sauce.
4. Toss cabbage with apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, and salt. Set aside for topping after baking.
5. Place flatbreads on sheet pans. Spread each with BBQ sauce, then top with mozzarella, cheddar, BBQ chicken, red onion, and bell pepper.
6. Bake 8 to 12 minutes, until the cheese melts and the flatbread edges are crisp.
7. Top with quick cabbage and green onions. Slice and serve.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Flatbread crispness and whether cabbage topping should stay or be replaced with pickled onions.
- Possible substitutions: Rotisserie chicken can make this a very fast Saturday meal.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Cook and shred chicken Friday or Saturday morning. Assemble flatbreads only right before baking.
- Family preference concerns: Use less BBQ sauce under the cheese if anyone dislikes a sweet pizza-style base.
