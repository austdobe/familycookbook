## Planning Summary

## Basic Instructions

1. Mix lemongrass, garlic, ginger, fish sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, oil, coriander, and black pepper. Coat pork and marinate 20 minutes, or up to 8 hours.
2. Start rice: rinse jasmine rice, then simmer with water and salt for 15 minutes. Rest covered 10 minutes.
3. Make quick pickles by stirring rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved. Add carrots, cucumber, and radishes. Let sit at least 20 minutes.
4. Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Cook pork in a thin layer until browned and cooked through, 2 to 4 minutes per side depending on thickness.
5. Slice pork if needed. Build bowls with rice, pork, drained pickled vegetables, herbs, and lime wedges.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Lemongrass strength and pickle sweetness.
- Possible substitutions: Pork tenderloin can be sliced thin and cooked quickly.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Make pickles Tuesday night. Marinate pork Wednesday morning.
- Family preference concerns: Fish sauce smells strong in the bottle but should taste savory and balanced after cooking.
