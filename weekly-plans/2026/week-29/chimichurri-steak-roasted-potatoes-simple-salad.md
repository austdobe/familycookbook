## Basic Instructions

1. Heat oven to 425 degrees F. Toss potatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast 30 to 35 minutes, turning once, until browned and tender.
2. Mix parsley, cilantro, garlic, jalapeno, red wine vinegar, lemon juice, olive oil, oregano, red pepper flakes, and salt. Let chimichurri sit while the steak cooks.
3. Pat steak dry. Rub with oil, salt, and pepper.
4. Grill over medium-high heat until browned and cooked to preferred doneness, about 4 to 6 minutes per side for flank steak. Aim for 130 to 135 degrees F for medium-rare or 140 to 145 degrees F for medium.
5. Rest steak 10 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
6. Toss romaine, tomatoes, and red onion with olive oil, lemon juice, and salt.
7. Serve sliced steak with chimichurri, roasted potatoes, and salad.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Chimichurri acidity and heat level.
- Possible substitutions: Sirloin steak works if flank or skirt is unavailable.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Chop chimichurri herbs Friday morning. Roast potatoes day-of for crisp edges.
- Family preference concerns: Slice against the grain or flank steak can feel chewy.
