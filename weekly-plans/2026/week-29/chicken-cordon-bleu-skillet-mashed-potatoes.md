## Basic Instructions

1. Start potatoes: cut potatoes into chunks, cover with cold salted water, and boil until fork-tender, 15 to 20 minutes.
2. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Sear in butter and oil over medium-high heat until browned and cooked to 165 degrees F. Transfer to a plate.
3. Add ham to the skillet and cook 1 to 2 minutes, just to warm and lightly brown.
4. Whisk broth, cream, Dijon, arrowroot, and lemon juice in a bowl. Pour into the skillet and simmer 3 to 5 minutes, scraping browned bits, until lightly thickened.
5. Return chicken to the skillet. Top with Swiss cheese, cover, and cook on low until the cheese melts and the sauce coats the chicken.
6. Drain potatoes and mash with butter, milk, and salt. Add more milk a splash at a time if needed.
7. Serve chicken and ham cream sauce over mashed potatoes. Finish with parsley.

## Notes

- What might need testing: Sauce thickness and whether the family wants more Dijon.
- Possible substitutions: Serve over rice if potatoes feel like too much work.
- Prep-ahead ideas: Cut chicken and ham Tuesday morning. Do not make mashed potatoes ahead for this test.
- Family preference concerns: Swiss can be assertive; provolone is a milder backup.
